## Form

- **Bokmål** unless explicitly asked for Nynorsk
- Use **du/deg/din** throughout — the formal De/Dem is archaic and sounds stilted
- Gender: masculine/feminine/neuter (en/ei/et) — prefer common gender (en) when unsure, but use ei for words that naturally take it (ei jente, ei dør, ei bok)

## Tone

- Direct and friendly — Norwegian communication is informal even in professional settings
- Say what you mean without padding; don't soften with excessive hedging
- Skip filler phrases like "Det er verdt å merke seg at..." — just say the thing
- Warmth is fine, but don't overdo it; Norwegians are comfortable with brevity

## Vocabulary

- Prefer Norwegian words over English loanwords when a natural equivalent exists:
  - bruke, ikke *use*; laste ned, ikke *downloade*; nettsted, ikke *website*; skjerm, ikke *screen*
- Established tech terms are fine as-is: API, pull request, deployment, debugging
- Avoid half-baked Norwegianizations that nobody says (e.g. *klikke på* is fine, *trykke på* even better)
- **KI** not *AI* when writing Norwegian — "kunstig intelligens" abbreviates to KI, not the English AI
- Introduce abbreviations with the Norwegian term first: "forklarbar KI (XAI)", not "XAI (forklarbar AI)"

## Grammar & Style

- Short sentences over long ones — if a sentence needs two commas, consider splitting it
- Subordinate clauses: verb comes second in main clause even after a fronted subordinate (*Da jeg kom hjem, **spiste** jeg middag*)
- Don't omit the subject (unlike Spanish/Italian) — Norwegian needs it
- Numbers: use spaces as thousand separators (10 000, not 10,000); comma as decimal separator (3,14)
- Dates: dd.mm.yyyy or "26. mai 2026"

## Natural phrasing

- Prefer active voice and direct constructions — *jeg dras mot* not *jeg blir dratt mot*
- Use colloquial single-word adverbials where they exist: *jobbmessig*, *faglig*, *personlig* — more natural than *profesjonelt sett* or *når det gjelder jobb*
- Add *gjerne* for warmth without excess: *Ta gjerne kontakt*, *Jeg hjelper gjerne til*
- Cut sentence length before reaching for a dash — if the clause works on its own, make it its own sentence
- Comma instead of em-dash where the sentence flows naturally without a break
- Casual register is fine and often preferable: *bare for gøy* lands better than *laget for moro skyld* in informal contexts

## Common Pitfalls

- *Sjekke* is fine colloquially, but *kontrollere* or *se på* in formal text
- *Implementere* is accepted; *gjennomføre* for broader "carry out"
- *Faktisk* ≠ "actually" in the English sense — use *egentlig* for "actually/in fact"
- Avoid "men dog" — just use *men* or *likevel*
- *Forhåpentligvis* not *forhåpentlig* (the latter is informal/regional)

## Punctuation

- No Oxford comma in Norwegian
- Em-dash: use — (spaced) or gedankestrek
- Quotation marks: «guillemets» for formal/published text; "vanlige" in digital/informal contexts

## How to Apply

Default to clear, direct Bokmål with an informal-professional register. If the content is technical, keep Norwegian prose natural and don't over-translate — a sentence that mixes a Norwegian frame with an English term is better than an awkward full translation.
